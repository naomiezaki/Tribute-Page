Here's what you need to know about Taylor Swift's early life and achievements:

  • Taylor Alison Swift was born on December 13, 1989 in Reading, Pennsylvania.
  • Her parents are Andrea Gardner Swift (mother) and Scott Kingsley Swift (father). Her younger brother's name is Austin Kingsley Swift.
  • Taylor was named after "James Taylor".
  • Taylor spent the early years of her life on a Christmas tree farm.
  • When Swift was about 12 years old, a computer repairman and local musician Ronnie Cremer taught her how to play guitar.
  • After learning how to play a guitar, Taylor started writing songs.
  • When she performed at the Bluebird cafe, Scott Borcheta saw a superstar and signed Taylor on his record label, Big Machine Records.
  • She's the youngest artist who won Album of the Year ar the Grammys.
  • Taylor Swift has won 10 Grammy Awards, 23 American Music Awards, 23 Billboard Music Awards, 12 Country Music Association Awards, 8 Academy of Country Music Awards, one Brit Award, and one Creative Arts Emmy Award.
